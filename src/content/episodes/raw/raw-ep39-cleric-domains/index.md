### Cleric Domains: Knowledge & Life

JT and Toby kick off their first long-distance episode diving into the diverse world of Cleric Divine Domains, starting with Knowledge and Life. Clerics are one of D&D's oldest classes, offering a wide array of specialized paths, each stemming from a god's "portfolio" of influence, like Apollo's options in knowledge, life, or light. Picking your domain is like finding the perfect match for your divine calling.

**Knowledge Domain**
This domain turns your cleric into a divine detective, focused on gathering and manipulating information.
*   **Spells:** Domain spells like Command, Identify, Augury, Suggestion, and Scrying provide tools for investigation and social influence. It's a roleplay heavy class, perfect for mystery or noir-themed campaigns.
*   **1st Level:** Gain proficiency in two languages and expertise in two skills chosen from Arcana, History, Nature, or Religion. This gives you two "super skills" right from the start, emphasizing the domain's non-combat focus.
*   **2nd Level (Channel Divinity: Knowledge of the Ages):** For 10 minutes, you gain proficiency with any skill or tool. This is super handy for filling gaps in the party's skills, especially for niche tool proficiencies that are often overlooked but can be crucial for downtime activities or tricky situations.
*   **6th Level (Channel Divinity: Read Thoughts):** You can read a creature's surface thoughts within 60 feet. If they fail a Wisdom save, you can then cast Suggestion on them for free, and they auto-fail the save. This offers significant social manipulation potential, great for interrogation or navigating court intrigue.
*   **8th Level (Potent Spellcasting):** Add your Wisdom modifier to the damage of your cleric cantrips. While a bit out of theme for this domain, it provides a small combat boost.
*   **17th Level (Visions of the Past):** Hold an object for a minute to learn its history or see visions of its immediate vicinity, up to a number of days equal to your Wisdom score. This is an incredible storytelling mechanic for DMs, allowing for deep investigations into an item's past and its previous owners, much like the game "Mystery on the Obra Dinn."

**Life Domain**
The ultimate iconic healer, this domain is for those who want to be the robust backbone of the party, both protecting and smashing.
*   **1st Level:** You gain proficiency in heavy armor, a major departure from the fragile healer trope. You're meant to be in the thick of combat.
*   **Spells:** A suite of powerful healing and protective spells including Bless, Cure Wounds, Lesser Restoration, Revivify, and Raise Dead. This domain is all about keeping your allies alive and fighting.
*   **Disciple of Life (1st Level):** Whenever you use a spell to restore hit points, the target regains an additional 2 + the spell's level in hit points. This significantly boosts the effectiveness of all your healing.
*   **2nd Level (Channel Divinity: Preserve Life):** Restore hit points equal to 5 times your cleric level, which you can divide among any creatures within 30 feet. This offers flexible, early-game healing on par with a Paladin's Lay on Hands.
*   **6th Level (Blessed Healer):** When you cast a 1st-level or higher spell to heal someone other than yourself, you regain hit points equal to 2 + the spell's level. This self-healing ensures you can stay in the fray while supporting your allies.
*   **8th Level (Divine Strike):** Deal an extra 1d8 radiant damage on a weapon attack, scaling to 2d8 at 14th level. Combined with heavy armor, this makes your Life Cleric a surprisingly potent front-line combatant.
*   **17th Level (Supreme Healing):** Whenever you roll dice to heal, you automatically use the maximum possible value for each die. This makes your healing incredibly consistent and powerful, transforming you into an unparalleled medic who can truly change the tide of battle. No one dies anymore!
